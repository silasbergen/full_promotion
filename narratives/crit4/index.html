Criterion 4: Student growth and development

Jan 10, 2024

During my time as Associate Professor, I contributed to student growth and development in the following ways:

  • I supervised student capstones and internships.

  • I wrote student letters of recommendation.

  • I served on a student Master’s committee.

  • I was invited to and participated in a student-led panel on healthy masculinity.

  • I led students on professional development trips.

For additional evidence of my support of student growth and development, please see notes of thanks from graduates

I served on a student Master’s committee.

Back to top

I did some consulting work for Will Varela, a master’s student in the biology department. This work centered on analyzing quality of the Whitewater river using biotic data on fish and invertebrates as well as stream characteristics such as temperature and quality of substrates on the river bed. Below are some of the plots I provided him to demonstrate different levels of quality (as represented by the three clusters; poor, moderate, and good) and their spatial distribution across three branches of the Whitewater River.

I was subsequently invited to serve on his Master’s thesis committee, to which I agreed. I participated in thesis draft review and sat in on his thesis defense.

I was invited to and participated in a student-led panel on healthy masculinity.

Back to top

In Fall 2019 a former STAT 110 student invited me to participate in a panel on healthy masculinity. The panel was hosted by the RE Initiative. I along with six other university faculty/staff/students responded to several questions posed by the RE Initiative student leaders. It was a valuable experience and I was honored to be invited and participate.

I led students on professional development trips.

Back to top

While traveling was paused for a couple years during COVID, it picked up a bit in the last couple years which was a welcome change. In September 2022, Dr. Chris Malone and I took students to a Twin Cities ASA chapter event Navigating difficult conversations with statistical collaborators. Many of the students who came were in my statistical consulting class, and enjoyed a rich and interactive conversation regarding potentially difficult communications with clients and collaborators.

Data analytics competitions, an invaluable learning experience for students, also took a pause with COVID but returned in Spring 2023 with a collaboration with the Minnesota Twins. MinneMUDAC brought in dozens of teams to analyze ticket sales data at Target Field with the goal of understanding factors that related to and predicting upcoming season prices. I was the faculty advisor for the 3 teams from Winona State that participated in the competition.

Silas Bergen
Associate professor of statistics and data science
