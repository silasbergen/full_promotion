Criterion 3: Continuing preparation

Jan 10, 2024

During my time as Associate Professor, I demonstrated continuing preparation in the following ways:

  • I took a class on the Python programming language.

  • I took the WeTeach course for effective online course design.

  • I read several books and online resources on data visualization design and best practices.

  • I sought out professional data visualizations as inspiration for DSCI 310 assignments.

I took a class on the Python programming language.

I took the WeTeach course for effective online course design.

Back to top

I have taught online multiple times and wanted to learn more about effective online course design, especially given the growth of online teaching especially amidst the COVID-19 pandemic. To that end I completed the WeTeach course for effective online/blended course design and delivery in Summer 2020.

One of the best takeaways from this class for me was to create a discussion board for students to introduce themselves at the beginning of the semester. I have done this in all my online offerings since taking WeTeach. I think students appreciate this chance to get to know one another, as it helps reduce the isolation that can be an issue in online courses. It also gives students a way to find people to work with in homework groups if they do not already know someone in the course.

I read several books and online resources on data visualization design and best practices.

Back to top

I have explored many resources in preparation for teaching DSCI 310: Data visualization. As described in Criterion 1, I wanted to remake the way I taught DSCI 310 by focusing more on the concepts of quality data visualization. This included expanding my treatment of visual perception and designing for aesthetics and accessibility. The following books and online resources have proved hugely instrumental to my course design and thinking on data visualization in general and DSCI 310 specifically:

  • Show me the Numbers by Stephen Few

  • Storytelling with Data by Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic

  • Storytelling with Data: Let’s practice! By Cole Nussbaumer Knaflic

  • The Truthful Art by Alberto Cairo

  • The Grammar of Graphics by Leland Wilkinson

  • #MakeoverMonday by Andy Kriebel and Eva Murray
