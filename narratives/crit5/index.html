Criterion 5: Service to the university and community

Jan 10, 2024

During my time as Associate professor, I served the university and community in the following ways:

  • I served on the organizing committee of SDSS 2019 and co-organized the data visualization track.

  • I designed the university COVID dashboard for reporting on the university COVID situation.

  • I served on the City of Winona’s COVID-19 community response team.

  • I directed the Statistical Consulting Center.

  • I served as a discussant for the Grant Writing Workshop Series.

  • I gave a college-wide presentation on my sabbatical research during STEM admissions day.

  • I served on several department and university-wide committees.

I served on the organizing committee of SDSS 2019 and co-organized the data visualization track.

Back to top

At the invitation of Kelly McConville, I co-organized the data visualization track at the 2019 Symposium for Data Science and Statistics in Bellevue, WA.

I agreed to serve on the organization committee, which entailed developing invited sessions and finding others to lead invited sessions. I organized two successful invited sessions, one on visual storytelling and one on data visualization education, both containing slates of high-quality speakers.

I designed the university COVID dashboard for reporting on the university COVID situation.

Back to top

In collaboration with the WSU communications staff, I designed a Tableau dashboard for reporting on the state of COVID-19 at the university. Specifically, the dashboard reported on the number of students isolating, quarantining, active cases and cumulative cases. I designed the dashboard so that internal staff could update it themselves every week without my assistance. This dashboard served an important part of the University’s communication on COVID-19, remaining on the website throughout the 2020-21 academic year.

I served on the City of Winona’s COVID-19 community response team.

Back to top

Through previous collaboration with city staff, I was invited to serve on the City of Winona’s COVID-19 community response team. My primary effort as part of this group was to create another dashboard that updated automatically on a daily basis showing the conditions of Winona County and surrounding counties. The City promoted this dashboard on its social media pages.
